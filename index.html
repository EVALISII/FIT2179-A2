The Underlying Crisis

Understanding Mental Health & Disability across Australia

Where is the burden highest?

Mental health service access varies significantly across Australia's states and territories, with Victoria recording the highest patient rate per 1,000 population in 2023-24.

A decade of growing demand

Medicare mental health services have grown steadily since 2014, peaking sharply during the COVID-19 pandemic before plateauing in recent years.

Which age groups have driven the decade of growth?

The 18-24 age group saw the sharpest rise during COVID-19 in 2020-21, reaching 178 patients per 1,000 — nearly double the rate of 55-64 year olds in the same year.

Who is seeking help?

Women aged 18-34 access Medicare mental health services at nearly double the rate of men in the same age group, highlighting significant gender differences in help-seeking behaviour.

Which providers are Australians turning to?

General practitioners remain the most common entry point for mental health care, though psychologists have seen the fastest growth over the past decade.

Are younger Australians being left behind?

Patient rates among 12-24 year olds have grown faster than any other age group since 2014, suggesting a youth mental health crisis that predates the pandemic.

Is demand consistent throughout the year?

Mental health service demand remains relatively stable across quarters, with a slight dip in the April-June quarter across most provider types.

More services, more emergencies

Despite record Medicare mental health services, emergency department presentations for mental health have also risen, suggesting the system is not reducing crisis-level need.

The urgency is escalating

The proportion of the most urgent mental health ED presentations — resuscitation and emergency has grown steadily since 2014, indicating more severe presentations over time.

What conditions drive people to emergency departments?

Mood disorders and anxiety-related conditions account for the largest share of mental health ED presentations, followed by schizophrenia and psychosis-related disorders.

Who is paying for mental health?

Federal and state government funding has grown substantially over the decade, though mental health spending still represents a small fraction of total health expenditure.

How does patient volume compare across states?

NSW and Victoria account for the largest total patient volumes, though Victoria has the highest rate per capita — suggesting stronger service access rather than simply larger population.

Does demand shift across the year?

Service demand dips in October to December across most provider types, with general practitioners showing the sharpest seasonal variation.

The gender gap in help-seeking

Women aged 18-34 access Medicare mental health services at nearly double the rate of men — a pattern that holds consistently across all working-age groups.
